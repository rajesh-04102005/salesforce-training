# Reflective Questions & Answers

## 1. Why can’t companies manage everything using Excel sheets?

Excel sheets become difficult when:
- Data grows large
- Multiple users work together
- Relationships are needed
- Security is required
- Reports become complex

Salesforce provides a structured and scalable solution.

---

## 2. Why are relationships important between objects?

Relationships connect related data.

Example:
- Students belong to Departments.
- Courses belong to Faculties.

Without relationships:
- Data duplication increases
- Reports become difficult
- Data consistency decreases

---

## 3. What problems happen if data is inconsistent?

Problems include:
- Wrong reports
- Duplicate records
- Incorrect decisions
- Customer confusion
- Data errors

---

## 4. Why should repetitive calculations be automated?

Automation:
- Saves time
- Reduces manual effort
- Prevents human mistakes
- Provides real-time updates

Example:
Percentage calculation using Formula Fields.

---

## 5. Why should invalid data be blocked early?

Blocking invalid data:
- Improves accuracy
- Prevents future errors
- Maintains clean databases
- Improves reporting quality

Example:
Validation Rule for negative age.

---

## 6. Why is Salesforce called a metadata-driven platform?

Salesforce is metadata-driven because:
- Most customization happens without coding.
- Objects, fields, relationships, and rules are stored as metadata.
- Applications can be configured visually.

---

# Final Reflection

Structured enterprise systems are important because they:
- Organize data properly
- Improve scalability
- Increase security
- Enable automation
- Improve business decisions
